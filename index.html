About This Project

This site serves as a central repository tracking the state of Optical Character Recognition (OCR) for Indic languages across the world. It aims to catalog research efforts, available datasets, tools, and resources to facilitate advancements in this domain.

Indic languages present unique challenges for OCR systems due to their complex scripts, diverse writing systems, and rich typographical traditions. This project aims to bridge the gap between research and implementation by providing a comprehensive overview of the field.

Datasets & Corpora

IIIT-Indic-HWR

A comprehensive dataset of handwritten text in 13 Indic scripts, containing over 20,000 handwritten document images.

Languages: 13 (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Bengali, Gujarati, Punjabi, etc.)
Size: 20,000+ document images
License: Research-only / By request

Digital Corpus of Sanskrit

A large digital corpus of Sanskrit texts, including digitized historical manuscripts and printed texts with ground truth.

Languages: Sanskrit

License: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0

Tools & Software

Tesseract OCR (Indic)

An extended version of Tesseract OCR with enhanced support for Indic scripts, including improved segmentation for conjunct characters.

Supported Languages: Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Gujarati, Punjabi
License: Apache 2.0
